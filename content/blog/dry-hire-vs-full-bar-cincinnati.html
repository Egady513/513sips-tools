What is Dry Hire Bar Service?

Dry hire (also called DIY bar or equipment-only rental) means you rent the bar structure, equipment, glassware, and professional bartenders, but you purchase all the alcohol, mixers, and supplies yourself. The mobile bar service provides everything needed to serve drinks professionally—you provide what's being served.

What's Typically Included in Dry Hire:

  • Mobile bar structure and setup
  • Professional bartenders (typically 1 per 50-75 guests)
  • Bar tools and equipment (shakers, strainers, jiggers, etc.)
  • Glassware or disposable cups
  • Ice bins and cooling equipment
  • Garnish trays and condiment dispensers
  • Setup and breakdown service
  • General liability insurance

What You Provide:

  • All alcoholic beverages
  • Mixers (tonic, soda, juices, etc.)
  • Garnishes (lemons, limes, olives, cherries, etc.)
  • Ice (though some services provide this)
  • Napkins and straws
  • Any specialty ingredients for signature cocktails

What is Full Bar Service?

Full bar service (also called wet hire or all-inclusive) means the mobile bar company provides everything—equipment, staff, alcohol, mixers, and all supplies. You choose a package or customize your selection, and the bar service handles every detail.

What's Included in Full Bar Service:

  • Everything from dry hire, plus:
  • All alcoholic beverages (beer, wine, liquor)
  • Non-alcoholic options
  • All mixers and garnishes
  • Ice and cooling
  • Menu planning and consultation
  • Recipe development for signature cocktails
  • Beverage purchasing and delivery
  • Responsible service training for staff
  • Liquor liability insurance
  • Permits and licensing (if required)

Comparing Costs: Dry Hire vs Full Bar

Understanding the financial differences helps you choose wisely:

Dry Hire Costs

Mobile Bar Rental: $800-1,500 (varies by bar style and guest count)

Alcohol & Supplies: $15-30 per person (depending on selection)

Total for 100 guests: Approximately $2,300-4,500

Note: You keep any unopened alcohol after the event, potentially reducing waste.

Full Bar Costs

Per-Person Packages: $25-55 per person

Total for 100 guests: Approximately $2,500-5,500

Note: Full service often includes premium options and eliminates the hassle of purchasing and transporting alcohol.

Pros and Cons: Dry Hire

Advantages of Dry Hire

  • Potential Cost Savings: Buy alcohol at wholesale or sale prices; keep leftovers
  • Complete Control: Choose exact brands, quantities, and varieties
  • Personal Touch: Source local or meaningful beverages
  • Transparency: Know exactly what everything costs
  • Flexibility: Adjust quantities up until the last minute

Disadvantages of Dry Hire

  • More Work: Research, purchase, and transport all beverages
  • Storage Challenges: Need space to store alcohol before the event
  • Waste Risk: Over-purchasing means dealing with excess after the wedding
  • Running Out: Under-purchasing creates an awkward situation
  • Permits & Licensing: You may need to handle certain legal requirements
  • No Professional Guidance: You're responsible for quantity calculations

Pros and Cons: Full Bar Service

Advantages of Full Bar

  • Stress-Free: Professionals handle every detail
  • Expert Guidance: Experienced advice on quantities and selections
  • No Waste: Bar service manages inventory; you don't deal with leftovers
  • Professional Recipes: Signature cocktails crafted by mixologists
  • Consistent Quality: Every drink made to professional standards
  • Legal Protection: Proper licensing and insurance handled by professionals
  • Backup Plans: If you run low, the bar service can restock

Disadvantages of Full Bar

  • Higher Cost: Convenience comes at a premium
  • Less Control: Limited to the bar service's offerings
  • Package Limitations: May include beverages you don't want
  • Markups: You pay for the service's time and expertise

Which Option is Right for Your Cincinnati Wedding?

Choose Dry Hire If:

  • You're working with a tight budget and want maximum control over costs
  • You have time and willingness to handle alcohol purchasing
  • You have specific brands or local products you want featured
  • You have storage space for pre-wedding alcohol
  • You have help transporting beverages to the venue
  • You're comfortable calculating quantities yourself
  • You want to keep leftover alcohol after the wedding

Choose Full Bar If:

  • You want a stress-free, hands-off experience
  • You value professional guidance and expertise
  • You're serving complex signature cocktails
  • You don't have time for alcohol shopping and logistics
  • You want professional liability coverage
  • You prefer predictable, all-inclusive pricing
  • You don't want to deal with leftovers

Hybrid Options: The Best of Both Worlds

Many Cincinnati mobile bar services, including 513 Sips, offer hybrid packages that combine elements of both approaches:

Popular Hybrid Models

  • You Buy Liquor, We Provide Rest: You purchase wine and spirits; bar service provides beer, mixers, and everything else
  • Consumption-Based: Bar service provides everything; you pay only for what guests actually drink
  • Minimum Guarantee: Flat fee up to a certain consumption level; overage charged separately
  • Limited Bar Packages: Full service but with curated selections (beer, wine, and 2-3 signature cocktails)

These options can offer the perfect balance of control and convenience.

Ohio-Specific Considerations

Cincinnati couples should keep these local factors in mind:

Liquor Laws

Ohio requires licensed bartenders for events serving alcohol. Professional mobile bar services ensure compliance, protecting you from liability. If doing dry hire, verify your bartenders hold proper licenses.

Local Favorites

Cincinnati has fantastic local breweries (Rhinegeist, MadTree, Braxton) and wineries. Whether you choose dry hire or full bar, featuring local options adds regional charm and often costs less than imports.

Seasonal Availability

Ohio's seasons affect beverage preferences. Full bar services often provide better guidance on seasonal selections, while dry hire gives you freedom to source limited-release local products.

Questions to Ask Your Mobile Bar Service

Before deciding, get clarity on these points:

  • What's included in your dry hire package vs. full service?
  • Can we customize a hybrid package?
  • Are there minimum guest counts for each option?
  • What happens if we run out of alcohol (dry hire) or exceed the package (full service)?
  • Do you help with quantity calculations for dry hire?
  • What insurance and permits do you carry?
  • Can we do a tasting before deciding on full service packages?
  • Are there additional fees for travel to our venue?
