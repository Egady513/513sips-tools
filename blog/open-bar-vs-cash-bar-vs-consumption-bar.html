Open Bar vs. Cash Bar
vs. Consumption Bar
— Which Is Right for You?

April 17, 2026  ·  By Eddie Gady · 513Sips

One of the biggest planning decisions for any event with alcohol is deceptively simple-sounding: how are guests paying — or not — for drinks? Get this wrong and you either blow the budget or leave guests feeling nickel-and-dimed at your own celebration. Here's a plain-English breakdown of every major bar setup, how Ohio law shapes your options, and exactly how dry-hire fits into each model.

Option 1: Open Bar

Bar Setup

The Host Picks Up the Tab

With an open bar, you — the host — purchase all the alcohol and the bartending service. Guests drink at no cost to them. It's the most celebratory setup and the gold standard for weddings and milestone events.

Pros

  • Guests feel genuinely taken care of
  • Sets a festive, generous tone for the event
  • No awkward payment moments at the bar
  • Easiest for guests to navigate

Watch For

  • Cost can run high without planning
  • Over-buying means money left on the table
  • No natural self-moderation by guests
How dry-hire helps: With 513Sips you buy your alcohol at retail — Total Wine, Costco, wherever you find the best price. There's no per-drink markup or venue liquor surcharge. We'll estimate quantities so you don't overbuy, and anything left at the end of the night goes home with you.

Option 2: Cash Bar

Bar Setup

Guests Pay for Their Own Drinks

A cash bar shifts the cost to your guests. It's common at corporate events, casual gatherings, and larger public events where a full open bar isn't practical or expected.

Pros

  • Very predictable cost for the host
  • Guests naturally moderate their own consumption
  • Appropriate for casual or professional settings

Watch For

  • Can feel inhospitable at weddings or family events
  • Slows service if guests are handling cash
  • Requires ticketing logistics (see below)
Important — Ohio Law: Dry-hire services like 513Sips operate without a liquor license, which means we cannot sell alcohol directly to guests. A traditional cash bar where guests pay per drink is not a legal option with a dry-hire service. Any "cash bar" arrangement must instead use a ticketing or drink token system managed by you, the host — see Option 4 below for how that works.

Option 3: Consumption Bar

Bar Setup

You Pay Only for What Gets Poured

A consumption bar means the host fronts the alcohol but only "pays" for what's actually consumed — because anything unopened or leftover comes back to you. It's the natural operating model of dry-hire: you buy the inventory, we pour it, you keep whatever's left.

Pros

  • No wasted spend on leftover alcohol
  • Great for smaller, more intimate events
  • Natural fit for dry-hire — zero markups
  • Feels just as generous as an open bar to guests

Watch For

  • Requires accurate quantity estimation up front
  • Running dry mid-event if underestimated
How dry-hire helps: This is where dry-hire shines. We help you estimate the right quantities based on guest count, event length, and drink preferences — so you buy just enough. Whatever's left at the end of the night is yours to take home or save for your next party.

Option 4: Ticket / Drink Token System

Bar Setup

The Best of Both Worlds

Each guest receives a set number of drink tickets (typically 2–3) included with their invitation or at check-in. Once those are used, they can purchase additional tickets from a designated ticket booth or table managed by you or an event volunteer — not the bartender.

Pros

  • High cost predictability — you cap your exposure
  • Guests still feel welcomed with complimentary drinks
  • Scales well for large events (100+ guests)
  • Fully legal with dry-hire in Ohio

Watch For

  • Needs a ticket-sales volunteer or table
  • Slightly more logistics to set up
  • Some guests may feel the limit is too low
How dry-hire helps: We work seamlessly with ticket systems. Guests hand us their ticket, we pour — simple. The host controls how many additional tickets are sold and at what price, entirely separate from our service. It's a clean, professional setup we've done many times.
